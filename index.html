üí° How It Works (The Simple Version)

üó∫Ô∏è What Are We Doing?

We're simulating a Range Query - one of the most common operations in spatial databases. It's like asking:

  • "Find all restaurants within 5 blocks of me"
  • "Show all buildings inside this city block"
  • "Which trees are in this park area?"

The app draws a random rectangular search area and finds ALL buildings inside it!

üì¶ What You See:
  • Red dots = Buildings/points on the map
  • Black lines = Quadrant boundaries
  • Blue dashed box = Search area (range query)
  • Yellow highlighted areas = Quadrants being checked
  • Gray dashed areas = Quadrants skipped (don't overlap)
  • Orange dots = Buildings found inside the search area
üöÄ Why It Matters:

Instead of checking every building when searching, a Quad Tree splits the map into smaller squares. Think of it like organizing your closet: instead of digging through everything, you divide it into sections (shirts, pants, shoes) and only look where needed!

Without Quad Tree: Search 10,000 buildings = 10,000 checks

With Quad Tree: Search 10,000 buildings = ~20 checks

It's used in GPS, video games, and computer graphics to make things 500x faster!
