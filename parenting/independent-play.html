üéØ What is Independent Play?

Independent play is when your child plays by themselves - without you needing to entertain them every second!

It's not about ignoring your child. It's about giving them space to explore, create, and problem-solve on their own.

üí° Independent play is a SKILL that develops over time. It's not something kids just "do" - we have to nurture it!

üõ†Ô∏è How to Encourage Independent Play

üì¶ Rotate Toys

Too many toys = overwhelm. Keep some packed away, rotate every few weeks. "New" toys spark interest!

üè† Create a Yes Space

A safe area where they can explore without you saying "no" constantly. Baby-proofed, open-ended toys.

ü™ë Start Close, Then Back Away

Sit nearby at first. Gradually increase distance. "I'm right here reading while you play."

üö´ Less is More

Simple toys (blocks, scarves, boxes) encourage creativity. Battery toys do all the work FOR them.

‚è∞ Make It Routine

"After snack is quiet play time." Consistency helps them know what to expect.

üëÄ Don't Interrupt!

If they're focused, don't comment or praise. Let the flow continue. Watch quietly.

ü§´ The Janet Lansbury Approach (RIE)

Janet Lansbury's RIE philosophy emphasizes respectful observation:

  • Watch, don't direct: Let them figure out how to use toys
  • Trust their play: Their play doesn't need to be "educational" or "right"
  • Sportscasting: Narrate what you see without judgment ("You put the block on top. It fell down.")
  • Accept boredom: Boredom leads to creativity. Don't rescue them from it!
"The greatest gift we can give children is our confidence in their ability to figure things out." ‚Äî Janet Lansbury

‚ùì But My Child Won't Play Alone!

This is super common. Here's what might be happening:

  • They're used to being entertained: Takes time to unlearn! Start with just 2-3 minutes.
  • Too many options: Simplify the environment. Fewer toys = deeper play.
  • Connection tank is empty: Fill their "cup" with 10-15 minutes of your full attention FIRST, then try independent play.
  • They're not developmentally ready: Some kids need more co-regulation before they can self-entertain. That's okay!
üí° "Connection before independence" - kids can't be independent until they feel securely connected!
