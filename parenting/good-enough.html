üéØ What Does "Good Enough" Mean?

The "Good Enough" concept comes from pediatrician and psychoanalyst D.W. Winnicott and has been expanded by modern experts.

The idea: You don't need to be a PERFECT parent. You just need to be "good enough" - present, responsive, and willing to repair when things go wrong!

üìä Research shows kids with "good enough" parents (not perfect, just present and trying) grow up just as healthy and happy as kids with "perfect" parents!

üîß What is Repair?

Repair is what happens AFTER a rupture (a disconnection, a conflict, a moment you didn't handle well).

The Repair Formula:

  • Notice: "I didn't handle that well"
  • Name it: "I got frustrated and yelled"
  • Take responsibility: "That's my stuff, not yours"
  • Reconnect: "I love you no matter what. Let's try again."
"The rupture-repair cycle is how children learn that relationships can survive conflict. That people can make mistakes AND still love each other." ‚Äî Dr. Becky Kennedy

üö´ Myths That Hurt Parents

‚ùå "I must never lose my temper"

Reality: Getting angry is human. Kids need to see that adults have emotions too.

‚úÖ Model: "I'm feeling frustrated. I'm going to take some deep breaths."

‚ùå "My childhood trauma will ruin my kids"

Reality: Awareness and healing break cycles. Your kids can have a different story.

‚úÖ "I'm working on my stuff so I can show up differently for them."

‚ùå "I need to do everything 'right'"

Reality: There's no one right way. Every family is different.

‚úÖ Trust your instincts. You know your child best.

‚ùå "If my kid is upset, I failed"

Reality: Kids have big emotions. It's not your job to prevent all sadness.

‚úÖ Your job: Help them THROUGH feelings, not AWAY from them.

üíö The Good Enough Checklist

You're doing great if you:

  • ‚úÖ Show up most of the time (not all the time!)
  • ‚úÖ Apologize when you mess up
  • ‚úÖ Make sure their basic needs are met
  • ‚úÖ Show them love even when they're difficult
  • ‚úÖ Try to understand their perspective
  • ‚úÖ Take care of yourself too (self-care isn't selfish!)
üéÅ The greatest gift to your child isn't perfection - it's a parent who's trying, growing, and repairing!

üßò Parental Self-Care is Part of It

You can't pour from an empty cup! Good enough parenting INCLUDES:

  • Setting boundaries around your needs
  • Taking breaks when overwhelmed
  • Asking for help
  • Having identity outside of being a parent
  • Being kind to yourself when you struggle
