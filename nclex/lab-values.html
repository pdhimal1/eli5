ELI5: Why Lab Values Matter

Lab values are like a window into what's happening inside the body. As a nurse, you'll be checking these values to see if patients are healthy or if something's wrong!

TL;DR: Know the normal ranges for common labs - you'll see these on the NCLEX!

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Values - Call Provider IMMEDIATELY!

  • Potassium: < 2.5 or > 6.5 mEq/L
  • Sodium: < 120 or > 155 mEq/L
  • Glucose: < 50 or > 400 mg/dL
  • WBC: > 30,000 or < 2,000
  • Platelets: < 20,000
  • troponin: Any elevated level
  • ABG pH: < 7.2 or > 7.6

üí° NCLEX Tips

  • Know potassium, sodium, and glucose ranges - most tested!
  • Understand what HIGH or LOW values mean for the patient
  • Critical values = immediate action needed
  • Look at trends - one value alone may not tell the whole story
