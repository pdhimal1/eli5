ELI5: Pharmacology for Nurses

Think of pharmacology as learning the "personality" of each medication. Just like you need to know if someone is friendly or grumpy, you need to know if a drug is helpful or dangerous in certain situations!

üîë The Big Categories You MUST Know

Cardiovascular

  • Antihypertensives: Lower blood pressure (Beta blockers, ACE inhibitors, Diuretics)
  • Anticoagulants: Prevent blood clots (Heparin, Warfarin)
  • Antiplatelets: Aspirin, Clopidogrel
  • Cardiac glycosides: Digoxin - watch for toxicity!

Pain & Inflammation

  • Opioids: Morphine, Oxycodone - watch for respiratory depression!
  • NSAIDs: Ibuprofen, Naproxen - watch kidneys and stomach
  • Acetaminophen: Tylenol - watch liver! Max 4000mg/day

Antibiotics

  • Penicillins: Amoxicillin - watch for allergic reactions
  • Macrolides: Azithromycin (Z-pack)
  • Fluoroquinolones: Ciprofloxacin - avoid with tendons
  • Aminoglycosides: Gentamicin - watch kidneys and hearing

Mental Health

  • SSRIs: Prozac, Zoloft - watch for serotonin syndrome
  • Benzodiazepines: Ativan, Xanax - addiction risk!
  • Antipsychotics: Haloperidol - watch for extrapyramidal symptoms
  • Mood stabilizers: Lithium - watch sodium levels!

Diabetes

  • Insulin: Rapid, short, intermediate, long-acting
  • Oral agents: Metformin, Glipizide

The "Five Rights" of Medication Administration:
  1. Right patient
  2. Right drug
  3. Right dose
  4. Right route
  5. Right time

Before Giving Any Medication - ALWAYS:

  • Check allergies!
  • Verify the order
  • Know the purpose
  • Know side effects
  • Assess before and after

üíâ IV Medication Tips

  • Central lines: Higher risk - double check everything
  • IV push: Know the rate (e.g., "over 2 minutes")
  • IV piggyback: Must be compatible with primary solution
  • Watch for infiltration: Pain, swelling, cool skin

üö´ Key Drug Interactions & Contraindications

  • Aspirin + Warfarin: Increased bleeding risk
  • Potassium + ACE inhibitors: Risk of hyperkalemia
  • Grapefruit + many drugs: Increases drug levels
  • Alcohol + CNS depressants: Respiratory depression
  • NSAIDs + ACE inhibitors: Kidney damage
