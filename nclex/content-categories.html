ELI5: The Test Blueprint

Think of the NCLEX test plan like a recipe. Just like a cake needs specific amounts of flour, sugar, and eggs, the NCLEX has specific "ingredients" - and some ingredients appear more than others!

38-54% of exam

Physiological Integrity is your biggest ingredient - almost half the exam!

28-40%

üõ°Ô∏è Safe and Effective Care Environment

This category is about keeping patients safe and working well with the healthcare team. It's divided into two parts:

Coordinated Care (18-24%)

  • Prioritizing patient care
  • Delegating tasks appropriately
  • Collaborating with healthcare team
  • Managing patient admissions/transfers/discharges
  • Following ethical guidelines
  • Client rights and advocacy

Safety and Infection Control (10-16%)

  • Standard precautions (handwashing, PPE)
  • Isolation procedures
  • Accident/error prevention
  • Hazardous material handling
  • Restraint use and safety
  • Fire safety (RACE, PASS)
6-12%

üíö Health Promotion and Maintenance

Helping people stay healthy and prevent illness! This includes:

  • Growth and development across the lifespan
  • Disease prevention (vaccinations, screenings)
  • Health education and promotion
  • Nutrition and lifestyle counseling
  • Early detection of health problems
  • Rehabilitation and chronic care
9-15%

üß† Psychosocial Integrity

Caring for patients' emotional and mental health:

  • Therapeutic communication
  • Coping mechanisms and stress management
  • Mental health disorders (depression, anxiety, bipolar, schizophrenia)
  • Substance abuse and addiction
  • End-of-life care and grief
  • Cultural and spiritual considerations
  • Abuse and neglect recognition
38-54%

üíä Physiological Integrity

The largest portion! Divided into four subcategories:

Basic Care and Comfort (7-13%)

  • Activities of daily living (bathing, dressing, feeding)
  • Mobility and positioning
  • Pain management
  • Nutrition and hydration
  • Elimination (bowel/bladder)
  • Comfort measures

Pharmacological Therapies (10-16%)

  • Medication administration (routes, calculations)
  • Drug interactions and side effects
  • IV therapy and calculations
  • Pain medications and anesthesia
  • Patient education on medications

Reduction of Risk Potential (9-15%)

  • Diagnostic tests and lab values
  • Monitoring for complications
  • Perioperative care
  • Prevention of falls
  • Special precautions (aspiration, pressure injuries)

Physiological Adaptation (7-13%)

  • Illness management (diabetes, heart failure, COPD)
  • Fluid and electrolyte balance
  • Respiratory emergencies
  • Shock and sepsis
  • Wound care and healing

üìä How to Use This Info

Don't study everything equally! Focus more on:

  • High weight: Physiological Integrity (especially pharmacology)
  • Medium weight: Safe and Effective Care Environment
  • Lower weight: Health Promotion and Maintenance
üí° Study Tip: Make sure you're strong in both "big" categories (Physiological Integrity and Safe/Effective Care) - together they make up about 70% of the exam!
