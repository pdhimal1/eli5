The beginning of your amazing journey!

ELI5: What's Happening?

Think of your baby like a tiny seed planted in soil. During the first trimester, that seed takes root and starts growing from just a few cells into a recognizable little human with a heartbeat!

TL;DR: Your body is working overtime to build the placenta (baby's support system) while baby grows from microscopic to about the size of a lime!

üçº What's Going On With Baby?

  • Weeks 1-4: Fertilization happens, embryo implants in uterus, begins dividing rapidly
  • Weeks 5-8: Heart starts beating! Arm and leg buds appear, neural tube (future brain & spinal cord) forms
  • Weeks 9-12: All organs formed, fingers and toes develop, baby starts moving (though you can't feel it yet)

ü§¢ Common Symptoms

Not everyone experiences these, but they're very normal:

  • Morning sickness: Actually can happen any time of day! Caused by rising hormone levels
  • Fatigue: Your body is working HARD growing a baby
  • Breast tenderness: Hormones preparing for breastfeeding
  • Frequent urination: Growing uterus pressing on bladder
  • Food aversions/cravings: Normal and temporary!
  • Mood swings: Hormones affecting emotions
Pro Tip: Eat small, frequent meals to help with nausea. Ginger tea or candies can help too!

üè• Your First Prenatal Visit

Usually happens around weeks 8-12. Here's what to expect:

  • Confirmation of pregnancy (maybe ultrasound!)
  • Review of medical history
  • Blood tests (blood type, immunity, infections)
  • Pelvic exam and pap smear
  • Discussion of prenatal vitamins
  • Lots of questions answered!

‚ö†Ô∏è What to Avoid

  • Alcohol: No safe amount during pregnancy
  • Smoking: Completely stop - it's dangerous for baby
  • Certain foods: Raw fish, unpasteurized cheese, deli meats (listeria risk)
  • High-mercury fish: Shark, swordfish, king mackerel
  • Cat litter: Risk of toxoplasmosis (get someone else to change it!)
Coffee: Good news! Research shows up to 200mg caffeine daily (about one 12-oz cup) is fine!

üíä Prenatal Vitamins

Start these as soon as you know you're pregnant (or even before!):

  • Folic acid: 400-600 mcg - prevents neural tube defects
  • Iron: Helps prevent anemia
  • Calcium: For baby's bones
  • Vitamin D: Helps body absorb calcium

üéâ The Good News

By the end of this trimester:

  • Miscarriage risk drops significantly (to about 1%)
  • Morning sickness often improves
  • You'll have your first ultrasound and maybe hear the heartbeat!
  • You can announce your pregnancy to friends and family
