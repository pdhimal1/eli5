üë∂ Third Trimester (Weeks 27-40+)

The home stretch - getting ready to meet your baby!

ELI5: The Final Countdown

Your baby is now basically fully formed and just getting bigger and stronger! It's like they're doing final practice runs for life outside the womb - breathing, sleeping, and even hiccupping!

TL;DR: Baby's lungs mature, you might feel uncomfortable, and labor could start any time after 37 weeks!

üë∂ What's Going On With Baby?

  • Weeks 27-30: Brain development speeds up, baby responds to sounds, may dream!
  • Weeks 31-34: Lungs nearly mature, gains weight rapidly, practice breathing
  • Weeks 35-37: Considered "early term" - almost ready!
  • Weeks 38-40: Full term! Baby should be head-down, ready to arrive

üòì Common Symptoms

The home stretch can be challenging:

  • Shortness of breath: Baby pressing on your diaphragm
  • Heartburn: Baby pressing on stomach
  • Swelling: Feet, ankles, hands - elevate when possible
  • Hemorrhoids: Common - use stool softeners
  • Back pain: Extra weight and loosened ligaments
  • Braxton Hicks: "Practice" contractions - usually irregular
  • Sleep struggles: Hard to get comfortable!
  • Anxiety: Mixed emotions about labor and parenthood

üõí Getting Ready

  • Pack hospital bag: Aim to have it ready by week 36
  • Install car seat: Must be properly installed before baby arrives!
  • Set up nursery: Crib, changing table, supplies
  • Take childbirth classes: Many hospitals offer these
  • Create birth plan: Preferences for labor and delivery
  • Stock up: Essentials for after baby arrives

üöë When to Call Your Doctor

Call immediately if you experience:
  • Vaginal bleeding or fluid leaking
  • Regular contractions before 37 weeks
  • Severe headache or vision changes
  • Sudden swelling in face/hands
  • Decreased baby movements
  • Any concerns - never hesitate to call!

ü©∫ Weekly Visits

From week 36 onward, you'll likely see your provider weekly:

  • Blood pressure checks
  • Weight monitoring
  • Urine tests
  • Fundal height measurement
  • Group B strep test (weeks 36-37)
  • Cervical checks (optional - not always done)

üéØ Signs of Labor

  • Water breaking: Could be a gush or trickle
  • Regular contractions: Getting stronger, more frequent, don't stop
  • Bloody show: Mucus plug dislodging (can happen days before labor)
  • Nesting instinct: Sudden burst of energy!
Remember: First babies often come late! Due dates are just estimates - your baby will come when ready!
